I am a current CPR instructor that has started a club in UCR that helps others get CPR certified and pass knowledge through quality basic life support classes. Not only does it allow me to have leadership skills, it also allows me to have interpersonal skills. The mission of my club is as follows. The mission of the Medical Emergency Education Project at the University of California, Riverside, is to ultimately improve the health of the community here in the Inland Empire by providing them with the resources to do so in the form of health education and outreach. As a health service provider, my main goal is to improve the health of the community and its individuals. But how do I do so? By being able to professionally hand out certification or training that comes with being a physician, an EMT, a nurse, or other professional careers. But as an instructor, we still have a significant role in alleviating the health issues in communities by giving others the resources to improve their own health and the ones around them. Especially, the main thing I am trying to pass along is knowledge!

By providing individuals with the knowledge of these important health issues and providing them with the skills needed to proficiently tackle the situations that are connected to those health issues, this enables them to better serve others both now as students and later on as potential workers in the healthcare field. Those who choose to pursue a career in health or medicine may even end up serving in the Inland Empire which is a reflection of how MEEP can truly have a long-lasting impact on this community. We can provide the knowledge for them to make healthier lifestyle decisions, to inform their friends and family of health issues, and to give them the skills to potentially save a life. Information is power: when we equip others with the right information pertaining to health, we improve the health of the community as they can use that information to better their lives or help others.

The Medical Emergency Education Project pursues this mission through two objectives: Education and Outreach. Through our BLS and First Aid classes, and through our health workshops at high schools, at UCR, and with on and off-campus organizations, by providing the younger generation with the firepower to utilize the knowledge they receive and put it into practical applications like changing their lifestyle, talking with others about health, or even pursuing a career in the healthcare field. This contributes to solving the long-term issues of health by turning facts into action.
